This application allows you to interactively browse global satellite imagery within hours of it being acquired. Use the features described below to find interesting imagery, save and share what you find, and download the underlying data.



Use the Layer Picker on the left to choose the type of imagery to display on the map. It also has a tab to download the underlying data.

Use the Timeline on the bottom to choose the date of the observations.

Use the Tool Bar at the top to see other tools for changing and saving the view.

Use the Map itself to pan or zoom in on an area.

Do not show again (Access from menu)

  1. Timeline

    Use the timeline below to explore satellite imagery that is available in the archive.

    The slider shows you where the selected date lies along the timeline. Click and drag the slider to quickly sweep through a range of dates.

    Click directly on the timeline to jump to a specific day. A blue bar indicates a layer has imagery available. The bar will be gray if the layer has been turned off and is not visible.

    Click on either "Days", "Months", or "Years" to expand or compress the range shown on the timeline.


    Try It!

    • Change the interval to "Months" and click on July, 2012
    • Change the interval to "Days" and explore the imagery for that month.

  2. Timeline - Continued

    To the left of the timeline the selected day is shown.

    Click the left and right arrows to quickly go to the previous or next day.

    Buttons above each portion of the date allow you to quickly go to the previous or next day, month, or year. Click on the text to type in a specific date.

    Try It!

    • Click on the button above the month to go to August.
    • Click on the day text, type in "23", and press the return key.

  3. Layer Picker - Base Layers

    A Base Layer is an opaque background image - you can have multiple active base layers, but you can only show one at a time.

    There are several ways to interact with the layers:

    • Use the icon to show and hide layers.
    • Use the icon to remove layers.
    • Drag the layers to rearrange them.

    Try It!

    • Click on the for "Corrected Reflectance (True Color) Aqua / MODIS" under Base Layers.
    • Drag "Corrected Reflectance (True Color) Aqua / MODIS" to the bottom of the list. Notice how the map changes.
    • Drag "Corrected Reflectance (True Color) Aqua / MODIS" back to the top of the list.

  4. Layer Picker - Continued

    An Overlay is a partially transparent layer to view on top of the background - you can view multiple overlays at once. Click on the button to adjust the opacity. If the overlay has a color bar, the palette can also be changed here.

    On the tab, you can use the drop down list or the search bar to find and add layers.

    On the tab, you can download data for layers you are currently viewing.

    Try It!

    • Click on the tab.
    • Type "aqua fires" in the search box.
    • Add the "Fires and Thermal Anomalies (Day and Night) Aqua/MODIS" overlay.
    • Click on the tab to see what data can be downloaded.
    • Click on the tab to see the layers that have been added.

  5. Map

    There are several ways you can interact with the map to pan or zoom.

    To pan, drag the map.

    To zoom:

    • Use the mouse wheel
    • Use the +/- icons on the right
    • Double-click (zooms to location)
    • Shift-click-drag (zooms in on a box)

    Try It!

    Move the map to North America and zoom in on northern California, USA.

  6. Toolbar

    The toolbar provides several additional utilities for interacting with @NAME@.

    The permalink button lets you create a permanent, shareable link to a particular view in @NAME@.

    The globe button lets you change between Arctic, geographic, and Antarctic projections of the world.

    The camera button lets you download an image of your current view in @NAME@. User-selected palettes are not yet supported with this feature.

    The information button provides you with more information on @NAME@ and its data sources.
